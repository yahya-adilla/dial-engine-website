ZLF SYSTEMS™ — DIAL ENGINE™ Deployment & Infrastructure Policy

1. Infrastructure Deployment

DIAL ENGINE™ is a managed communications and operational infrastructure platform developed and deployed by ZLF Systems. Deployment services include CRM environment configuration, routing architecture, automation provisioning, operational workspace setup, and backend infrastructure integration required to support scalable outbound sales operations.

Standard infrastructure deployment begins at $5,000 USD, which includes initial configuration, deployment engineering, and the first thirty (30) days of managed operational support.

2. Managed Service Structure

DIAL ENGINE™ is provided as a managed B2B infrastructure environment and not as a self-serve software product. Ongoing maintenance, backend optimization, automation updates, routing support, and operational monitoring are provided through the standard $3,000/month Managed Infrastructure Plan.

Infrastructure expansion requirements, increased operational scale, custom integrations, and additional active agent capacity may result in adjusted monthly support pricing depending on deployment scope.

3. Proprietary Systems & Client Data

All underlying deployment architecture, backend systems, automation logic, routing configurations, operational tooling, and proprietary infrastructure associated with DIAL ENGINE™ remain the exclusive intellectual property of ZLF Systems.

Clients retain ownership of their operational data, lead databases, customer records, and internal business information processed through their deployed infrastructure environment.

4. Operational Restrictions

Deployed infrastructure environments are licensed exclusively for the purchasing organization’s internal business operations. Infrastructure deployments, backend systems, operational templates, and proprietary deployment methodologies may not be duplicated, redistributed, sublicensed, resold, or transferred to external entities without written authorization from ZLF Systems.

5. Third-Party Infrastructure

Certain deployment environments may integrate with third-party telecommunications providers, cloud infrastructure vendors, database platforms, messaging systems, and external routing services required for operational functionality. Clients remain responsible for third-party carrier usage fees, SIP trunking expenses, and lead acquisition costs associated with their operations.
