ZLF SYSTEMS™ — DIAL ENGINE™ Standard B2B Deployment Terms

1. Infrastructure Deployment Provisions

Dial Engine™ provides deployable outbound communications infrastructure, operational CRM environments, routing systems, and scalable command architecture for enterprise sales organizations. By remitting the standard $5,000 Infrastructure Deployment Fee, the purchasing entity (the "Licensee") is granted a non-exclusive operational license to utilize the deployed systems strictly for internal business operations supporting up to fifty (50) active agents.

2. Proprietary Rights & Customer Data

The Licensor retains all right, title, and interest in and to the Dial Engine™ infrastructure stack, including all underlying software systems, backend logic, routing architecture, deployment methodologies, operational tooling, derivative works, and associated intellectual property rights. This Agreement grants a limited operational license and does not convey ownership of the underlying infrastructure to the Licensee. The Licensee retains ownership of all proprietary customer data, lead databases, and operational records processed through the deployed environment.

3. Managed Infrastructure & Operational Scaling

Continued backend maintenance, operational support, infrastructure optimization, automated updates, and deployment monitoring are provided through the standard $3,000 Monthly Managed Infrastructure Fee. This ensures continued operational stability and compatibility with integrated third-party telecommunications and cloud infrastructure providers.

Additional operational scaling beyond the standard fifty (50) active agent allocation is available at a rate of $500/month per additional 50 active agents. Enterprise-scale deployments exceeding one hundred fifty (150) active agents may require custom infrastructure agreements and adjusted operational support pricing.

4. Infrastructure Restrictions & Non-Transferability

This license is bound strictly to the original purchasing business entity. The deployed infrastructure, operational tooling, backend systems, deployment templates, routing configurations, and proprietary methodologies may not be duplicated, redistributed, sublicensed, resold, or transferred to external third-party organizations without the execution of a separate written licensing agreement with the Licensor.

5. Third-Party Service Providers

Certain operational environments may integrate with third-party telecommunications carriers, SIP trunking providers, cloud infrastructure vendors, messaging platforms, and database systems required for deployment functionality. The Licensee remains solely responsible for external telecommunications expenses, carrier billing, SIP usage charges, and lead acquisition costs associated with their operational environment.
