Infrastructure Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 2026

DIAL ENGINE™ and ZLF Systems prioritize operational data security and infrastructure isolation for all deployed client environments. We understand that lead inventory, routing systems, and workforce operations represent critical business infrastructure for high-ticket sales organizations.

1. Client Data Ownership

All lead databases, CRM environments, operational records, routing configurations, and client-generated business data remain the exclusive property of the purchasing organization. DIAL ENGINE™ does not claim ownership over client leads, sales data, or internal operational information processed through deployed infrastructure environments.

2. Infrastructure & Automation Operations

DIAL ENGINE™ functions as a managed operational infrastructure platform designed to provision CRM stations, automate workforce operations, route lead inventory, and maintain centralized floor management systems. Core automation processes operate without requiring the resale, harvesting, or monetization of client lead data.

3. Operational Metrics & Floor Analytics

To support floor management, leaderboard systems, infrastructure monitoring, and operational auditing, certain aggregate operational metrics may be processed internally, including dial activity, station status, transfer counts, and system utilization statistics. This information is utilized exclusively for client infrastructure operations and is not sold or distributed to external entities.

4. Access Control & Offboarding

DIAL ENGINE™ includes centralized access management systems designed to protect operational environments from internal misuse or unauthorized retention of lead inventory. Administrative controls allow management to revoke access, recycle operational stations, and secure assigned CRM environments during workforce offboarding procedures.

5. Third-Party Infrastructure Providers

Certain deployments may integrate with third-party telecommunications carriers, SIP trunking providers, cloud infrastructure vendors, database systems, and messaging platforms required for operational functionality. Client organizations remain responsible for external telecommunications expenses, carrier billing, SIP usage charges, and independently acquired lead inventory.
